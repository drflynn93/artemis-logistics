Solar Logistics
Space supply chain strategy — Moon base, Venus cloud city, and the race for the solar system

The mission

2025–2032. NASA's Artemis and China's Chang'e race to the lunar south pole for water ice — the key to rocket fuel manufactured off-Earth. By year 7, the most ambitious logistics challenge in history opens: Venus orbit. At 52km altitude, pressure and temperature are Earth-like. HAVOC cloud platforms can mine sulfur dioxide for synthetic fuel and gather phosphine — possible evidence of life. The question isn't whether to go. It's whether your supply chain can get you there first.

🇺🇸 Artemis (You)

Lead NASA. Select commercial partners, manage cargo and crew, deploy ISRU, court ESA and JAXA as risk-sharing partners, and reach Venus.

🇨🇳 Chang'e (AI)

Autonomous, methodical, well-funded. Launches once per year. Gets faster as tension rises. First to ISRU wins the fuel economy.

🇪🇺 ESA (Year 3)

Offers Ariane 6 capacity and risk-sharing on ISRU in exchange for 30% of your fuel stockpile rights. Accept or compete.

🇯🇵 JAXA (Year 3)

Offers south pole data and 25% cost reduction on supply missions in exchange for joint base naming rights. Accept or compete.

Game structure

Y1
Years 1–2: Moon supply missions. Build relationship with SpaceX, Boeing, or Blue Origin. Race China to ISRU.
Y3
Year 3: ESA and JAXA diplomacy moment. Accept risk-sharing partnerships or compete solo.
Y4
Years 4–6: Expanded Moon operations. Coalition members share cost and risk. Build fuel stockpile for Venus transit.
Y7
Year 7: Venus unlocks. Deploy HAVOC aeroshell platforms at 52km altitude. Mine SO₂ and phosphine. Build Aphrodite Station.

Year 3 — Diplomatic overtures

Two agencies have approached Artemis with partnership proposals

China's progress has alarmed both ESA and JAXA. Each agency is offering a different kind of risk-sharing partnership. You can accept both, one, or neither — but declining means they may eventually align with China instead.

🇪🇺 European Space Agency — Ariane 6 partnership

ESA offers dedicated Ariane 6 launch slots and absorbs 35% of cost overruns on your next 3 missions in exchange for 30% rights to your lunar fuel stockpile. The joint base becomes the "Artemis-ESA Lunar Gateway."

🇯🇵 JAXA — Lunar south pole data sharing

JAXA shares SLIM lander data on south pole ice deposits (reducing your ISRU risk modifier by 20%) and contributes $15M per supply mission in exchange for joint base naming rights and 1 Japanese crew slot per mission.
